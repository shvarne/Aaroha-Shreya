Our Events

A se ah

About: ‘A se Ah’ is an annual event conducted under Project Anand by Aaroha. It recreates the spirit of an annual school function for the underprivileged children associated with the organization offering them an experience filled with joy and learning. Volunteers contribute their creativity and passion to organize activities such as morning assemblies, group meals, quizzes, and games.

Read More

It makes ‘A se Ah’ a celebration of learning and togetherness. Mission: Aaroha envisions ‘A se Ah’ as a means to dispel the sense of inadequacy that often plagues children who lack access to traditional educational opportunities. We believe that the acceptance of inferiority is more damaging than actual deprivation, and through this event, we strive to foster a sense of equality and self-worth in every child.

Aakar

About: Initiated in 2014, Project Aakar aims at the holistic development of children. Each weekend, diverse activities are organized to equip them with multidisciplinary skills, such as technical and communication skills. A notable initiative under this project is ‘Bastishala,’a series of interviews where underprivileged children interact with prominent personalities.

Read More

Over 300 children have participated in Aakar, gaining valuable insights and experiences. Mission: As its name suggests, Aakar’s core aim is to shape the character, personality, and thought process of children. We strive to guide them in navigating the world around them, helping them become conscientious and informed members of society.

Read More

Aavishkar

Aavishkar, organized by Aaroha at Technosearch, was a successful science event where students showcased various science projects. Underprivileged children from the slums also participated, emphasizing the joyful aspect of learning. Aarohians provided mentorship, elevating the quality of these projects. The event also featured engaging games and activities.

Read More

Aavishkar aimed to promote scientific temperament in children and simplify scientific concepts through projects. The event included interactive displays, educational workshops, and decorations reflecting the theme of inventions. The implementation involved ensuring attendee engagement, scheduling workshops, arranging venue space, setting up a registration system, and providing necessary materials. The event began with an opening ceremony and included judging sessions.The event's success was evident through the scientific insights gained by students, the admiration expressed by the audience, and the rich experiential learning opportunities provided.

Fundraising events

About: Every year, on Independence Day (15th August) and Republic Day (26th January), Aaroha organizes fundraising events across different locations in Bhopal. These events are designed to reignite the spirit of patriotism and encourage individuals to contribute to the nation’s betterment. Our volunteers, driven by their passion, engage the public to raise funds.

Aasha

About: Conducted under Project Anand, Aasha is a series of events and activities that provide children from underprivileged communities with opportunities to explore and showcase their talents. This platform allows them to share their skills and gain new experiences, fostering confidence and personal growth.

Unmilanam

About: In a rapidly developing country, it is crucial for young minds to remain connected to their roots. Unmilanam is an event designed to celebrate the essence of the Indian education system. A distinguished guest is invited to speak about the importance of education, encouraging children to delve deeper into learning and appreciating the richness of our educational heritage.

Read More

The mission of Unmilanam is to keep children informed and engaged with the values and traditions of the Indian education system, ensuring they remain connected to their cultural and educational roots.
